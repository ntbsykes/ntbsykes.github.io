About Me

I am a student of metapopulation ecology and evolutionary biology. My research interests lie in population genetics, speciation, hybridization, and conservation.

Outside the lab, I'm dad to my two cats and can often be found riding my bike uphill or looking at birds.

Leveraging genomics to better manage our wild populations and cultivars

I have always had a deep fascination with living things. Growing up in the mountains of British Columbia, I was lucky to observe my fair share of charismatic megafauna. In these forests and alpine zones, I also saw plenty of disturbance. My passion is firmly directed toward populations most affected by anthropomorphic change.

"...a keen naturalist, lab technician and data analyst."

My academic journey had its roots in this passion; I sought to protect wild spaces. Studies in environmental science quickly gave way to ecology, where population dynamics were dictated by harvesting practices and habitat loss. I have spent the years building out my biologist's toolkit as a keen naturalist, lab technician and data analyst. Now, I am lucky to work with Dr. Gregory L. Owens at the University of Victoria, where we use next generation sequencing technology to elucidate patterns of gene flow in wild populations.
